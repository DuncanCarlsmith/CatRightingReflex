Cat Righting Reflex

The cat righting reflex is the ability of cats and many other animals to reorient during a fall to land on their feet, starting from rest with zero angular momentum. (Wikipedia)

The model. Two identical cylinders (or half-cylinders) of radius R and length L are joined at a fixed bend angle θ. A no-wringing constraint requires equal and opposite spin rates +ω (front) and −ω (rear) about their geometric axes. The spin angular momentum of each half about the system center of mass is SF and SR. Because the axes are not parallel, ST = SF + SR points along the x-axis. Conservation of angular momentum requires L = −ST: the whole body counter-rotates about x. This is the righting flip. An impulsive torque τ at the joint initiates the spins; the equal and opposite reaction −τ acts on the other half.
